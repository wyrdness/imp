# Sources & References

## Books

### Medieval & Historical Sources
- Medieval grimoires and demonology texts (various). Primary sources on demonic hierarchies and imps.
- Church writings on demons (Medieval period). Theological discussions of minor demons.

### Folklore Collections
- Briggs, K.M. (1976). *An Encyclopedia of Fairies: Hobgoblins, Brownies, Bogies, and Other Supernatural Creatures*. Pantheon Books. ISBN: 978-0394409184
- Briggs, K.M. (1967). *The Fairies in English Tradition and Literature*. University of Chicago Press.

### Witch Trial Sources
- Witch trial records (1500s-1600s). English and Scottish trials documenting imps as familiars.
- Hopkins, M. (1647). *The Discovery of Witches*. Historical witch-finder manual.
- Various witch trial pamphlets (16th-17th centuries). Contemporary accounts of imp familiars.

### Demonology
- Weyer, J. (1563). *De Praestigiis Daemonum* (On the Illusions of Demons). Classical demonology text.
- Scot, R. (1584). *The Discoverie of Witchcraft*. Skeptical examination of witchcraft beliefs.
- King James VI/I. (1597). *Daemonologie*. Royal treatise on demons and witchcraft.

### Academic Studies
- Russell, J.B. (1984). *Lucifer: The Devil in the Middle Ages*. Cornell University Press.
- Roper, L. (2004). *Witch Craze: Terror and Fantasy in Baroque Germany*. Yale University Press.
